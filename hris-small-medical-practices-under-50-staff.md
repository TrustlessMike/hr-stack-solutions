# HRIS for Small Medical Practices Under 50 Staff

Medical practices under 50 staff need HR software that handles provider credentialing, multi-location scheduling, and healthcare compliance without enterprise complexity. This guide identifies the best HRIS platforms for small medical practices, from single-provider offices to multi-specialty groups.

## The Small Medical Practice HR Challenge

Medical practices under 50 staff face unique HR requirements:

- **Provider credentialing:** Medical licenses, DEA, board certifications, payer enrollment
- **Multiple staff types:** Physicians, NPs, PAs, nurses, MAs, front desk, billing, coding
- **Shift complexity:** Clinical rotations, provider call schedules, front desk coverage
- **Healthcare compliance:** HIPAA, OSHA, CLIA, state medical board requirements
- **Revenue cycle impact:** Hiring delays affect patient access and reimbursement
- **Multi-location:** Many practices operate 2-5 clinic locations
- **Seasonal hiring:** Flu shot clinics, vaccination drives, temporary clinical staff

Generic SMB HRIS platforms miss these medical practice workflows.

## Critical Features for Medical Practice HRIS

### Provider Credential Management

Your HRIS must track provider credentials:
- Medical license by state (MD, DO, NP, PA)
- DEA registration and expiration
- Board certification status
- Medicare/Medicaid enrollment
- Commercial payer enrollment
- Hospital privileges tracking
- CME completion records
- Malpractice insurance coverage

### Clinical Shift Scheduling

Medical practices run extended hours. Your scheduling needs:
- Provider schedule templates (clinic days, OR days, admin days)
- Call schedule rotation management
- Clinical staff rotation (nurses, MAs)
- Front desk coverage scheduling
- Float pool management across locations
- Integration with patient scheduling systems
- Coverage forecasting for patient volume

### Multi-Location Management

Many practices operate multiple clinics. Require:
- Cost center tracking by location
- Location-specific time rules
- Transfer tracking between sites
- Location-based reporting
- Multi-location org chart
- Location-specific onboarding checklists

### Provider Compensation Tracking

Provider pay differs from staff. Your system needs:
- RVU-based compensation tracking
- Collections-based bonus calculation
- Salary + productivity models
- Call stipend management
- Locum tenens tracking
- 1099 contractor management

### Healthcare Compliance

Medical practices face strict compliance. Ensure:
- HIPAA-compliant data handling
- OSHA training tracking
- Bloodborne pathogens certification
- CLIA certification tracking (if applicable)
- State medical board compliance
- Vaccination and health screening records

## Medical Practice Compliance Risks

Common audit triggers for small practices:

### Provider Credential Gaps
- **Risk:** Provider seeing patients with expired license or DEA
- **Penalty:** Practice fines, potential malpractice non-coverage
- **Prevention:** Automated alerts 90-60 days before expiration

### HIPAA Violations
- **Risk:** Employee PHI exposed in HR system
- **Penalty:** $100-50,000 per violation
- **Prevention:** HIPAA-compliant HR vendor with BAA

### Labor Law Violations
- **Risk:** Missing break requirements for clinical staff
- **Penalty:** Back pay + penalties up to $10,000
- **Prevention:** Automated time tracking with state rules

### Worker Classification
- **Risk:** Misclassifying providers as 1099 vs. W-2
- **Penalty:** Back taxes + penalties
- **Prevention:** Clear classification rules in HRIS

## Medical Practice HRIS Selection Framework

Follow this decision framework to choose the right platform:

### Step 1: Count Your Provider Types
- **Single provider:** Gusto or BambooHR sufficient
- **2-3 providers:** Paycor or Paylocity recommended
- **4+ providers:** Paycor or ADP Run for complexity
- **Mixed providers (MD, NP, PA):** Require credential tracking by type

### Step 2: Map Your Location Structure
- **Single location:** Any platform works
- **2-3 locations:** Prioritize multi-location features
- **4+ locations:** ADP Run or Paycor for enterprise features
- **Multi-state:** Require multi-state compliance

### Step 3: Audit Your Compensation Complexity
- **Salary only:** Basic payroll sufficient
- **Salary + bonus:** Require bonus tracking
- **RVU-based:** Require RVU calculation integration
- **Collections-based:** Require practice management integration
- **Mixed models:** Require flexible compensation engine

### Step 4: Identify Integration Needs
- **Practice management:** eClinicalWorks, AthenaHealth, Epicom
- **Billing/coding:** Integrated billing workflows
- **Accounting:** QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite
- **Benefits carriers:** Health, dental, malpractice insurance

### Step 5: Calculate True TCO
Don't just compare PEPM. Calculate 3-year total cost:
- **Software subscription:** Monthly × 36 months
- **Implementation fees:** One-time setup costs
- **Add-on costs:** Time tracking, credential modules
- **Integration costs:** Practice management connectors
- **Support costs:** Premium support tiers

## Medical Practice Credential Management Guide

Follow this guide for complete credential compliance:

### Provider Credential Categories
1. **Medical Licensure:**
   - MD/DO license by state
   - DEA registration
   - Controlled substance license (state-specific)
   - Board certification status

2. **Mid-Level Licensure:**
   - NP license by state
   - PA license by state
   - Collaborative practice agreements
   - Prescriptive authority

3. **Payer Enrollment:**
   - Medicare PTAN
   - Medicaid NPI
   - Commercial payer contracts
   - ACA marketplace participation

4. **Hospital Privileges:**
   - Admitting privileges
   - Consulting privileges
   - Procedural privileges
   - Telemedicine privileges

5. **Continuing Education:**
   - CME completion tracking
   - MOC (Maintenance of Certification)
   - State CME requirements
   - Specialty CME requirements

### Credential Alert Workflow
1. **120 days out:** Initial renewal reminder to provider
2. **90 days out:** Office manager notification
3. **60 days out:** Escalation to practice administrator
4. **30 days out:** Suspension workflow initiated
5. **Expired:** Practice management system flag

### Credential Documentation Requirements
- Primary source verification
- Copy of current license
- DEA certificate
- Board certification certificate
- Malpractice declarations page
- CME completion certificates
- Hospital privilege letters

## Medical Practice Compensation Tracking

Provider compensation varies significantly. Configure for your model:

### Salary-Based Compensation
- **Base salary:** Fixed annual amount
- **Quality bonuses:** Value-based care metrics
- **Patient satisfaction:** Press Ganey scores
- **Administrative stipend:** Committee work, leadership

### RVU-Based Compensation
- **Work RVU:** Provider work component
- **Practice expense RVU:** Overhead allocation
- **Malpractice RVU:** Liability component
- **Conversion factor:** $/RVU rate
- **Threshold:** Minimum RVU guarantee

### Collections-Based Compensation
- **Professional fees:** CPT code reimbursement
- **Facility fees:** Place of service differential
- **Payer mix:** Medicare, Medicaid, commercial
- **Denial rate:** Claims rejection tracking
- **Days in A/R:** Collection speed metric

### Hybrid Models
- **Salary + RVU:** Base + productivity
- **Salary + collections:** Base + collections %
- **Tiered RVU:** Increasing $/RVU at thresholds
- **Capitation:** PMPM (per member per month)

## Final Recommendation

For most small medical practices under 50 staff, **Paycor** delivers the best balance of healthcare-specific features, credential tracking, and multi-location support. The medical practice workforce management capabilities justify the investment. The provider compensation tracking and practice management integration address real practice challenges.

For single or two-provider practices needing simple payroll, **Gusto** provides affordable core functionality with fast implementation. The HIPAA-compliant data handling and BAA availability work for small practices.

For practices prioritizing provider experience and retention, **BambooHR** offers industry-leading self-service with manual credential tracking. Pair with Gusto for payroll to get best of both worlds.

For multi-location practices (3+ sites), **ADP Run** provides enterprise-grade compliance and scalability. The multi-state features and provider compensation complexity handling justify the investment.

For practices wanting modern user experience, **Paylocity** delivers mid-market features with consumer-grade UX. The employee engagement surveys and peer benchmarking add value.
