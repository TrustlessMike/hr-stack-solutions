# HR Software for Urgent Care Centers 50-100 Employees

Urgent care centers with 50-100 employees need HR software that handles provider credentialing, multi-location scheduling, and 24/7 shift coverage. This guide identifies the best HRIS platforms for growing urgent care groups and retail health clinics.

## The Urgent Care HR Challenge

Urgent care centers face unique HR requirements:

- **24/7 scheduling:** Many urgent cares operate evenings, weekends, holidays
- **Provider credentialing:** Emergency medicine credentials, DEA, hospital privileges
- **Multi-location:** Urgent care groups operate 3-15 centers
- **Acuity-based staffing:** Patient volume drives staffing levels
- **Extended hours:** Front desk and clinical staff work non-traditional hours
- **Locum coverage:** Provider gaps require temporary coverage
- **Retail health integration:** Some urgent cares co-locate with retail pharmacies
- **Fast growth:** Urgent care groups often expand rapidly (5-10 new centers/year)

Generic healthcare HRIS platforms miss urgent care-specific workflows.

## Critical Features for Urgent Care HR Software

### 24/7 Shift Scheduling

Urgent care runs extended hours. Your scheduling needs:
- 24/7 coverage templates (days, evenings, nights, weekends)
- Holiday scheduling and coverage
- Provider shift rotation management
- Clinical staff rotation (RNs, MAs, X-ray techs)
- Front desk coverage for extended hours
- Float pool management across centers
- Integration with patient volume forecasting

### Provider Credential Management

Emergency medicine credentials require tracking:
- Medical license by state (MD, DO, NP, PA)
- DEA registration and expiration
- Board certification (Emergency Medicine, Family Practice)
- Medicare/Medicaid enrollment
- Hospital privileges tracking
- ACLS, PALS, BLS certification
- CME completion records
- Malpractice insurance coverage
- Urgent care credentialing (UCAOA standards)

### Multi-Location Urgent Care Management

Urgent care groups operate multiple centers. Require:
- Cost center tracking by location
- Location-specific scheduling rules
- Transfer tracking between centers
- Location-based reporting (volume, staffing, costs)
- Multi-location org chart
- Location-specific onboarding checklists
- Regional manager dashboards
- New center opening staffing templates

### Acuity-Based Staffing

Patient volume drives staffing. Your system needs:
- Patient volume integration (visits per hour)
- Staffing level recommendations by acuity
- Real-time staffing adjustments
- Overtime alerts based on volume
- Float pool deployment tools
- Patient wait time correlation to staffing

### Extended Hours Compliance

Non-traditional hours create compliance complexity. Ensure:
- Shift differential calculations (evenings, nights, weekends)
- Holiday pay automation
- On-call scheduling and tracking
- Call-back pay management
- Break compliance for extended shifts
- State-specific emergency staffing laws

## Urgent Care Compliance Risks

Common audit triggers for urgent care centers:

### Provider Credential Gaps
- **Risk:** Provider seeing patients with expired DEA or license
- **Penalty:** Center fines, potential malpractice non-coverage
- **Prevention:** Automated alerts 90-60 days before expiration

### Emergency Staffing Laws
- **Risk:** Violating state emergency staffing requirements
- **Penalty:** State health department fines
- **Prevention:** Automated staffing compliance tracking

### Extended Hours Labor Law
- **Risk:** Missing break requirements for extended shifts
- **Penalty:** Back pay + penalties up to $10,000
- **Prevention:** Automated time tracking with state rules

### Multi-State Compliance
- **Risk:** Urgent care group operating across states without compliance
- **Penalty:** State labor law violations, tax penalties
- **Prevention:** Multi-state HRIS with automatic compliance updates

## Urgent Care HRIS Selection Framework

Follow this decision framework to choose the right platform:

### Step 1: Count Your Provider Types
- **Single provider per center:** Basic credential tracking sufficient
- **2-3 providers per center:** Require robust credential management
- **Mixed providers (MD, DO, NP, PA):** Require license tracking by type
- **Locum coverage:** Require temporary provider onboarding

### Step 2: Map Your Center Structure
- **Single center:** Any platform works
- **2-5 centers:** Prioritize multi-location features
- **6+ centers:** ADP or Paycor for enterprise features
- **Multi-state:** Require multi-state compliance

### Step 3: Audit Your Scheduling Complexity
- **Standard hours (8am-8pm):** Basic scheduling sufficient
- **Extended hours (7am-9pm):** Require shift differential tracking
- **24/7 operations:** Require 24/7 scheduling engine
- **Holiday coverage:** Require holiday pay automation
- **On-call scheduling:** Require call schedule management

### Step 4: Identify Integration Needs
- **EHR system:** athenaClinicals, Meditech, eClinicalWorks
- **Patient scheduling:** Integrated appointment data
- **Accounting:** QuickBooks, Xero, practice-specific GL
- **Benefits carriers:** Health, dental, malpractice insurance

### Step 5: Calculate True TCO
Don't just compare PEPM. Calculate 3-year total cost:
- **Software subscription:** Monthly × 36 months
- **Implementation fees:** One-time setup costs
- **Add-on costs:** Time tracking, credential modules
- **Integration costs:** EHR connectors
- **Support costs:** Premium support tiers

## Urgent Care Credential Management Guide

Follow this guide for complete urgent care credential compliance:

### Urgent Care Credential Categories
1. **Physician Licensure:**
   - MD/DO license by state
   - DEA registration
   - Controlled substance license (state-specific)
   - Board certification (Emergency Medicine, Family Practice)

2. **Mid-Level Licensure:**
   - NP license by state
   - PA license by state
   - Collaborative practice agreements
   - Prescriptive authority

3. **Nursing Credentials:**
   - RN license by state
   - LPN license by state
   - ACLS certification
   - PALS certification
   - BLS certification

4. **Allied Health Credentials:**
   - X-ray tech certification
   - Lab tech certification
   - MA certification
   - Phlebotomy certification

5. **Compliance Training:**
   - HIPAA training (annual)
   - OSHA bloodborne pathogens (annual)
   - Infection control (annual)
   - State-specific mandated training

### Credential Alert Workflow
1. **120 days out:** Initial renewal reminder to provider
2. **90 days out:** Office manager notification
3. **60 days out:** Escalation to center director
4. **30 days out:** Suspension workflow initiated
5. **Expired:** EHR system access flag

### Credential Documentation Requirements
- Primary source verification
- Copy of current license
- DEA certificate
- Board certification certificate
- Malpractice declarations page
- CME completion certificates
- Hospital privilege letters (if applicable)

## Urgent Care 24/7 Scheduling Guide

Urgent care centers operate extended hours. Configure for your model:

### Shift Pattern Categories
1. **Standard Shifts:**
   - Day shift: 7am-3pm, 8am-4pm
   - Evening shift: 3pm-11pm, 4pm-midnight
   - Night shift: 11pm-7am, midnight-8am

2. **Extended Hour Shifts:**
   - Early morning: 6am-2pm
   - Late evening: 2pm-10pm
   - Weekend shift: 8am-8pm Sat/Sun

3. **Holiday Shifts:**
   - Holiday coverage: Essential staff only
   - Holiday premium: 2x pay rate
   - Holiday scheduling: 30 days advance notice

4. **On-Call Shifts:**
   - Provider call: Home call for backup
   - Admin call: Operations manager on-call
   - IT call: System support on-call
   - Call stipend: Fixed per-call payment

### Shift Differential Calculations
- **Evening differential:** +10% base rate (after 6pm)
- **Night differential:** +15% base rate (after 10pm)
- **Weekend differential:** +10% base rate (Sat/Sun)
- **Holiday differential:** +100% base rate (holidays)
- **On-call stipend:** Fixed $200-500 per call period

### Coverage Forecasting
- **Patient volume:** Visits per hour by day of week
- **Staffing ratio:** Providers per X patients
- **Acuity level:** High-acuity requires more staff
- **Seasonal patterns:** Flu season requires surge staffing
- **Weather impact:** Weather affects patient volume
